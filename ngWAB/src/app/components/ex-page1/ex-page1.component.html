Rubiks Cube



For centuries, people have been obsessed with puzzles. Ever since humans have been able to think they have been obsessed with the idea of pushing the boundaries of thier minds and problem solving abilities. The Rubiks Cube is the latest in the series.

A Rubiks Cube is a puzzle built with 26 cubes and 54 visible sides that each contain pne of six colors. The goal of an individual attempting to solve a Rubiks cube is match all of the cubes of the same color and have them together on the same face of the overall puzzle as the rest of the cubes by twisting and turning the cube until your goal is achieved.

This appears simple at first, but becomes more complex as you work through the puzzle, as you have to maintain the current face you have 'solved' while continuing to finish matching together the other faces of the puzzle. This is accomplished through various algorithmns, and there is no one solution. Many people can solve the Rubiks Cube in various ways, with some of the faster and more common methods having names.

While the Rubiks Cube is popular, it is far from being a challenge in the current day and age. Many individuals can solve the Rubiks Cube, and in the day of the internet those who can't can simply use a search engine such as google or bing in order to find out.

This problem is not limited to the Rubiks Cube, as more and more individuals are just looking up the answers online It is a good solution, as it saves many hours of frustration of not being able to solve a puzzle which already has a solution. However, this is not a good thing for puzzles, as they tend to not present the same challenge or excitement that they once had.

Is this the end of puzzles? I say no. There are still plenty of people in the world who want to see puzzles succeed and still enjoy the thrill of going through and solving a puzzle that they previously didn't have the answer to. These are the individuals who will keep puzzles going regardless of the number of tutorials of how to solve them online, or the increasing number of people turning to things like video games or their phones to entertain themselves instead. Puzzles will always have a place as long as there are people who are willing to and enjoy solving them
